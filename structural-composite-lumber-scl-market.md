<p><strong>The Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL)</li><li>Laminated Strand Lumber (LSL)</li><li>Parallel Strand Lumber(PSL)</li><li>Oriented Strand Lumber (OSL)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) includes engineered wood products made from layers of wood or strands, bonded together for enhanced strength and stability. Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL) consists of thin wood veneers glued together, ideal for beams and headers. Laminated Strand Lumber (LSL) uses wood strands to create large structural components. Parallel Strand Lumber (PSL) aligns strands in the same direction for high strength, often used in beams. Oriented Strand Lumber (OSL) interlocks strands in various orientations, suitable for structural panels and framing.</p></p>
