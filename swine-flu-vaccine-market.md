<p><p>The Swine Flu Vaccine market is categorized into different administration types: intradermal, intramuscular, subcutaneous, and others. Intradermal vaccination involves injecting the vaccine into the skin, promoting a robust immune response with a smaller dose. Intramuscular vaccination is the most common method, delivering the vaccine deep into the muscle for effective absorption. Subcutaneous vaccination administers the vaccine into the fatty tissue beneath the skin, offering a slower absorption rate. The "others" category may include alternative delivery methods, such as oral or nasal vaccines.</p></p>
